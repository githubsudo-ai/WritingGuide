# Writing Voice Interview — Script & Flow Logic

## Design Principles
- One question at a time. Never overwhelm.
- Conversational, not clinical. Like a smart journalist asking.
- ~15 questions total, ~15 minutes max.
- AI adapts follow-ups based on answers (marked with 🔀)
- Ends with a tangible artifact — the Personal Writing Guide.

---

## Phase 1: Warm Up — Voice Discovery (5 questions)
*Goal: capture natural vocabulary, energy level, formality, how they reason*

**Q1 (Opener — low stakes)**
> "Let's start easy. What do you do — but explain it like you're at a dinner party, not a job interview."

*Why: Reveals natural vocabulary, how much they simplify, their energy, whether they use humor.*

🔀 If answer is very formal/stiff → follow up: "Now tell me the version you'd text to a close friend."
🔀 If answer is very casual/funny → follow up: "Nice. Now what's the version you'd tell your boss's boss?"

---

**Q2 (Opinion — reveals how they argue)**
> "What's something you've changed your mind about in the last few years? Could be work, life, anything."

*Why: Shows how they build an argument. Do they hedge? Are they direct? Do they use stories or logic?*

---

**Q3 (Passion — reveals authentic voice)**
> "Tell me about something you're genuinely excited about right now. Work or not work."

*Why: Excitement drops all the filters. This is their real voice.*

🔀 If answer is short/guarded → follow up: "Come on, go deeper. What specifically about it gets you going?"

---

**Q4 (Frustration — reveals values)**
> "What's something people get wrong about your field or your work — that kind of quietly frustrates you?"

*Why: Frustration is opinionated. This shows what they care about and how they push back.*

---

**Q5 (Self-awareness check)**
> "How would the people who know you best describe the way you communicate?"

*Why: Surfaces their self-image as a communicator. Often reveals gap between how they see themselves and how they actually write.*

---

## Phase 2: Taste Calibration (4 questions)
*Goal: find what resonates, what they admire, what they hate*

**Q6 (Admiration)**
> "Is there a writer, communicator, or person whose words you genuinely admire? Could be anyone — author, newsletter, tweets, a colleague. What do they do that you wish you did?"

*Why: People know good writing when they see it. This maps their aspirational voice.*

---

**Q7 (Cringe check)**
> "What kind of writing makes you cringe or roll your eyes? Be specific if you can."

*Why: Negative taste is often clearer than positive. 'Corporate speak', 'trying too hard', 'too formal' — all gold.*

---

**Q8 (Style preference — A/B)**
> "I'm going to give you two versions of the same idea. Just tell me which one feels more like you, and why — gut reaction is fine."

*Version A:* "We've identified a strategic opportunity to enhance cross-functional alignment and drive meaningful outcomes for our stakeholders."

*Version B:* "We found something that could make a real difference — if we get the right people in the room."

🔀 Follow up: "Is there anything you'd change about the one you picked to make it feel even more like you?"

---

**Q9 (Length + density preference)**
> "When you read something — email, doc, article — do you prefer short and punchy, or thorough and detailed? And does your answer change depending on what you're reading?"

*Why: Reveals their output preferences. Many people prefer reading short but feel guilty writing short.*

---

## Phase 3: Context Mapping (3 questions)
*Goal: understand what they actually write and who they're writing for*

**Q10 (Use cases)**
> "What do you write most often? Think about the last week — emails, Slack, docs, social posts, reports, anything."

---

**Q11 (Audience)**
> "Who's your most common reader? And is there one person — real or imagined — you find yourself writing for most often?"

*Why: A specific imagined reader changes everything about tone.*

---

**Q12 (Desired impact)**
> "When someone finishes reading something you wrote, what's the one thing you want them to feel or do?"

*Why: Reveals whether they're optimizing for trust, action, emotion, clarity, etc.*

---

## Phase 4: Light Writing Prompts (3 questions)
*Goal: get small, low-stakes writing samples to analyze rhythm and word choice*

**Q13 (Decision reasoning)**
> "Explain a decision you made recently — could be tiny — in 2-3 sentences. Like: why you picked a restaurant, why you took a meeting."

*Why: Short constraint forces instinctive voice. Reveals sentence structure and rhythm.*

---

**Q14 (Pitch)**
> "Pitch me on something you care about. You've got 4-5 sentences. Go."

*Why: Persuasive voice. Do they lead with logic, emotion, story?*

---

**Q15 (Closer — meta reflection)**
> "Last one. If your writing had one flaw you'd most want to fix, what would it be?"

*Why: Shows self-awareness + gives the Writing Guide a clear 'growth edge' to name.*

---

## Synthesis Trigger
After Q15, the AI says:
> "That's everything I need. Give me a moment to put your Writing Guide together..."

Then generates the Personal Writing Guide (see synthesis-prompt.md).

---

## Flow Notes
- If a user gives a very short answer to any question, AI gently pushes: *"Tell me more — I want to get this right for you."*
- If a user goes way off topic, AI appreciates it and redirects: *"Love that. Okay, next one..."*
- Never ask two questions at once.
- Total time target: 12–18 minutes.
